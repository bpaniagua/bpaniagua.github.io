Medical Image Processing Research

I am a medical image processing researcher at Kitware. I am the lead of the craniomaxillofacial imaging research projects as well as the lead of the morphometric analysis projects. Craniomaxillofacial (CMF) refers to the different soft and hard tissues in the cranium (or skull) and the face. Those structures are very important; the cranium protects the brain and the face, sinuses, airways and teeth hold functions such as mastication or speech. The face is also the organ of emotion and it conveys huge amounts of information that guide our social interactions and our identity of self. Most diseases of the CMF complex have a psychological underbelly and cause disability, social stigma and chronic pain. Learn more about what I do at my NIH reporter pages (1, 2 and 3) and my Kitware personal page.

Women in Science

Degrees/careers in computing are hard, competitive and demanding. I think everybody going through the process of graduating from a computer science program has it hard at some point, regardless of gender or ethnicity. Women in computing often have it a bit harder due to the extra expectations and biases we face. I am no exception as a female computer scientist. Always that my family and career responsabilities allow me to, I try to be part of women in science events and initiatives. I encourage diversity by presenting my honest history as a girl in Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM). I have educated myself and have taken an active role in initiatives to promote retention of women in the workforce throughout their careers at my different job positions. I am outspoken and vocal about what it is to be a woman in science, and I have been a speaker at different girls and women in STEM events.
